One final, much better known, song, is “War” by Edwin Starr. Its lyrics explicitly express their intent. War, especially the Vietnam War, is good for nothing.

“War
I despise
It means destruction to innocent lives
War means tears
To thousands of mothers' eyes
When their sons go out to fight
And lose their lives”

Specifically, Starr puts the focus on the families turned upside down. Like others, Starr focuses on the innocence of war, that innocent, young people are sent to fight having no knowledge of what the war is for. “Imagine” by Lennon and “War” by Starr are played countless times again on the radio today, solidifiying the significance of the antiwar era of music.

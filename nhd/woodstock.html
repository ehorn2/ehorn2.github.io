Antiwar sentiment was also boosted by the infamous Woodstock festival. Dozens of artists performed for an audience of over 400,000. It was advertised as “a weekend of peace and ‘good vibrations’”. Mixed with hippies, “flower children”, and drugs, the event was a beacon for counterculture and the anti war cause, and further refined what the anti war genre of music was.


This poster shows the original advertising for the festival in 1969. It was advertised as, "3 Days of Peace and Music".

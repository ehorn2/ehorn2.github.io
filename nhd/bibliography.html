Blowin' in the Wind. By Bob Dylan, Columbia Records, 1963. This source provides an early example of 
     antiwar music with strong lyrics. This is a primary source, as it is directly from the era 
     being studied. 
Foner, Eric, and John A. Garraty. "Vietnam War." The Reader's Companion to American History, 1991. 
     SIRS Issues Researcher, explore.proquest.com/sirsissuesresearcher/document/ 
     2265467495?accountid=45026. This source provides some extra information about the Vietnam War. 
     It helped with this project as it gave me some context to factors of the war that are less 
     obvious through antiwar music. This is a secondary source, as it is a summary of the war from a 
     textbook. 
Fortunate Son. By John Fogerty, Fantasy Records, 1969. This primary source provides an example of 
     how antiwar music questioned the ethics of the draft for the Vietnam War. 
Grimstad, Paul. "Rewinding Jimi Hendrix's National Anthem." The New Yorker, Condé Nast, Jan. 2021, 
     www.newyorker.com/culture/cultural-comment/rewinding-jimi-hendrixs-national-anthem. Accessed 28 
     Jan. 2021. This source gave a lot of insight into how the public reacted to Hendrix's rendition 
     of the Star Spangled Banner. It gave me a much better understanding of how his performance at 
     Woodstock shaped counterculture and protest music. 
Imagine. By John Lennon, Apple Records, 1971. This source helped me widen the array of examples of 
     antiwar music. By including different artists across different genres, this song helped me 
     strengthen my points by giving me a strong, but varied example. 
Jimi Hendrix at the Woodstock Music Festival. 18 Aug. 1969. University of Michigan Arts and Culture, 
     arts.umich.edu/wp-content/uploads/sites/35/2019/07/ 
     Screen-Shot-2019-07-01-at-10.57.34-AM-720x540.png. This image of Jimi Hendrix performing at 
     Woodstock perfectly captured both Woodstock and the performance of his anthem. 
Meacham, Jon, and Tim Mcgraw. "The Music of American Politics." New York Times, 2019 Jun 16. SIRS 
     Issues Researcher, explore.proquest.com/sirsissuesresearcher/document/ 
     2294129635?accountid=45026. This article gave me an overall basic understanding of the 
     different artists and songs that were popular during the time. It helped me get a basis as to 
     what I should research further. 
Ragged Old Flag. By Johnny Cash, Columbia Records, 1974. This source was nuanced, as it beams 
     patriotism, while also criticizes the scandals of war. It provides yet another example of 
     antiwar music's effects on the war. 
"The Sixties and Protest Music." The Gilder Lehrman Institute of American History, 2019, 
     ap.gilderlehrman.org/history-by-era/sixties/essays/protest-music-1960s#_ftn2. Accessed 24 Jan. 
     2021. This source provided a basic understanding of the entire antiwar movement, especially in 
     the context of US History. 
Skolnick, Arnold. Woodstock Festival. 1969. New England Public Media, www.nepm.org/post/ 
     meet-designer-who-created-iconic-1969-woodstock-festival-poster#stream/0. Accessed 7 Mar. 2021. 
     This poster helps illustrate the Woodstock Festival and what it was all about, so it would be 
     useful to include in my project. 
War. By Edwin Starr, Gordy Records, 1970. This source provides one of the best examples of antiwar 
     music during the era. Starr's strong lyrics and passion while singing are a perfect example of 
     the genre, and support my thesis beautifully. 
Yanak, Ted, and Pam Cornelison. "Woodstock." The Great American History Fact-Finder, 1993. SIRS 
     Issues Researcher, explore.proquest.com/sirsissuesresearcher/document/ 
     2265817656?accountid=45026. This source gave me information about the Woodstock Festival, which 
     would be great for characterizing the counterculture movement in a context that supports the 
     antiwar movement.

Unfortunate Son

“Unfortunate Son” discusses the flawed ethics of the war while bringing a rock groove.

“It ain't me, it ain't me
I ain't no senator's son, son
It ain't me, it ain't me
I ain't no fortunate one”

He also states, “I ain’t no millionaire’s son”, and “I ain’t no military son,” calling into the ethics of the draft. Creedence Clearwater Revival’s hit also questions false showings of patriotism that surrounded the war.

Yeah, some folks inherit star-spangled eyes
They send you down to war
And when you ask 'em: "How much should we give?"
They only answer: "More, more, more"

Those claiming to work for the benefit of the country in symbols of patriotism were sending people exclusively of the poorer, less fortunate class, into battle to die. The song pointed out this unfairness and inequality to the masses, which flared up antiwar sentiment.
